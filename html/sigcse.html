Theses on how SIGCSE can be problematic

  • The SIGCSE community lacks transparency and accountability in decision-making processes.
  • The SIGCSE community should center the voices and experiences of underrepresented groups in computer science education.
  • The SIGCSE organization should be more open to feedback and criticism from its members and the wider community.
  • How has the SIGCSE conference changed over time, and what factors have contributed to these changes?
  • How can the SIGCSE community work to address the ethical and social implications of computer science education in a meaningful and responsible way?
  • What steps can the SIGCSE community take to address academic elitism and make the conference more accessible to non-traditional educators and students?
  • How can the SIGCSE conference better incorporate critical thinking and social responsibility into its curriculum and presentation topics?
  • What alternative models of computer science education can SIGCSE explore to promote innovation and inclusivity?
  • How can the panel review process be adjusted to better incorporate diversity of thought?
  • The SIGCSE has become opposed to the iconoclasm which is inherent in computer science
  • How can SIGCSE better engage with and elevate the voices and perspectives of underrepresented groups in computer science education?
  • In what ways can the SIGCSE aim to achieve wider participation in computer science?
  • What can SIGCSE do to promote cultural humility and sensitivity in its conference proceedings and materials?
  • The SIGCSE conference's resistance to change and innovation hinders the growth and development of computer science education as a dynamic and evolving field.
  • SIGCSE's lack of attention to the intersectionality of identities, such as race, gender, and sexuality, in computer science education perpetuates exclusion and discrimination in the field.
  • SIGCSE's lack of attention to the role of computer science education in shaping ethical and moral values contributes to a tech industry that prioritizes profit over social responsibility.
